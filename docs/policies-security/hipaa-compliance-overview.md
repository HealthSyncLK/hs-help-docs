# HIPAA Compliance Overview

Learn how HealthSync maintains compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).

## What is HIPAA?

The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) is US federal law that:
- Protects health information privacy
- Sets security standards for health data
- Regulates healthcare information disclosure
- Establishes patient rights
- Requires safeguards for electronic health records

## HealthSync's HIPAA Status

### Covered Entity or Business Associate?

**HealthSync as Business Associate**
- Acts on behalf of covered entities
- Handles Protected Health Information (PHI)
- Signs Business Associate Agreements (BAA)
- Maintains HIPAA compliance

**You as Individual**
- Personal health records outside HIPAA when self-managed
- HIPAA applies when sharing with covered entities
- Your rights protected regardless

### Business Associate Agreement (BAA)

Available for:
- Healthcare providers
- Covered entities
- Business associates
- Enterprise customers

Request BAA: enterprise@healthsync.com

## Protected Health Information (PHI)

### What is PHI?
Information that:
- Relates to health condition
- Relates to healthcare provision
- Relates to healthcare payment
- Can identify an individual

### How We Protect PHI

**At Rest**
- AES-256 encryption
- Separate encryption keys
- Key management system
- Encrypted backups

**In Transit**
- TLS 1.3 encryption
- Certificate pinning
- Secure APIs
- VPN for admin access

**In Use**
- Access controls
- Audit logging
- Secure deletion
- Data minimization

## Your HIPAA Rights

### Right to Access
- View your PHI
- Get copies
- Electronic format
- Without unreasonable delay

### Right to Amend
- Request corrections
- Add statements
- Explanations of disputes
- Tracking of amendments

### Right to Accounting
- Who accessed your PHI
- When and why
- For what purpose
- Last 6 years

### Right to Restrict
- Limit uses and disclosures
- Request restrictions
- We may not always agree
- Must honor reasonable requests

### Right to Confidential Communications
- Choose how we contact you
- Alternative contact methods
- Protected addresses
- Confidential messaging

### Right to Notice
- Receive notice of privacy practices
- Changes to practices
- How we use PHI
- Your rights explained

### Right to Complain
- File complaints about violations
- No retaliation
- To HealthSync or HHS
- Investigation of complaints

## Breach Notification

### What is a Breach?
Unauthorized acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI that:
- Compromises security or privacy
- Poses significant risk
- Not covered by exceptions

### Our Obligations

**Discovery to 60 Days**
- Investigate breach
- Assess risk
- Determine affected individuals
- Notify all parties

**Individual Notification**
- Within 60 days
- Written notification
- Email if preferred
- Contact information
- Description of breach
- Steps we're taking
- What you should do

**HHS Notification**
- Large breaches (500+): Immediately
- Small breaches: Annual report
- Website posting if media notice

### Your Response
- Change passwords
- Monitor accounts
- Review access logs
- Enable 2FA
- Report suspicious activity

## Enforcement and Penalties

### If We Violate HIPAA
Potential consequences:
- Civil penalties ($100-$50,000 per violation)
- Criminal penalties (up to $250,000 and 10 years)
- Corrective action plans
- Reputation damage

## Frequently Asked Questions
